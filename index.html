From the Authors

The speed of change of AI technology is rapid. Managers are worried, workers are scared, parents are concerned, and policymakers are confused. This situation may last for a long time, and we write this book to help them make better decisions and discover new opportunities.

The future is not shaped by technology alone. Whatever the timelines, understanding the consequences of AI requires economic reasoning about scarcity, about complementarities and bottlenecks, about incentives, and about the organisation of work. These are the concepts we use. Our specific examples will age — these concepts will not.
